# General Meeting Etiquette

We are an all-remote company which means lots of meetings! Following the guidelines below helps us remain productive whilst organizing or participating in meetings.

#### As the Organizer:&#x20;

* All meetings should have an Agenda and Objectives / Success criteria (the attendees don’t have to attend if these aren’t included).&#x20;
* If someone hasn’t responded, then assume they’re not attending (or they may attend, but that’s not confirmed).
* Be conscious of people's diaries - always check in Google Calendar prior to booking a meeting.&#x20;
* Google Meet is used for all meetings.&#x20;
* Feel free to add a virtual notetaker to external meetings (as long as it's not confidential), but don’t take offense if people want to exclude it - we have a love-hate relationship with them!&#x20;
* Avoid recurring meetings where possible - they clog up diaries!&#x20;
* Respect your participant's time and keep comments brief and to the point.&#x20;
* Leave enough time at the end for wrap up and next steps without running over.

#### As an Attendee:&#x20;

* It is okay to leave a meeting politely if it’s not relevant or you’re not getting value from it.&#x20;
* Show up on time and come prepared.&#x20;
* Come to the meeting with a positive attitude.&#x20;
* Be patient when listening to others speak and do not interrupt them.
* If you feel like you can’t jump into a conversation, raise your (virtual) hand.&#x20;
* Attack the problem, not the person.&#x20;
* Respectfully challenge the idea, not the person.&#x20;
* If you are going to be more than a couple of minutes late, please let the organizer know.&#x20;
* We all have bad hair days, but we encourage everyone to have their cameras on (it can be very difficult to speak to a blank screen).
