# Secure software development practices

## Secure-by-design principles:

The **Secure by Design** principles guide the development of software and systems that are **secure from the ground up**. Their core purpose is to **prevent vulnerabilities** and **minimize damage** in the event of an attack by embedding security into every stage of the design and development process.

**Goal:** Create systems that are **resilient, robust, and harder to compromise** by default.

## Privacy-by-design principles:

The **Privacy by Design** principles focus on embedding **privacy protection** into technologies, processes, and business practices **from the start**. The aim is to ensure that individuals’ personal data is **handled with care, transparency, and respect** throughout its lifecycle.

**Goal:** Build systems that **protect personal data by default**, giving users confidence, control, and trust in the services they use.

<table data-header-hidden><thead><tr><th width="205"></th><th width="186"></th><th></th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>Principle</strong></td><td><strong>Summary</strong></td><td><strong>Explanation</strong></td></tr><tr><td><strong>Proactive not Reactive; Preventative not Remedial</strong></td><td>Anticipate and prevent privacy issues</td><td>Build systems to prevent privacy breaches before they happen, rather than reacting to them afterward.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Privacy as the Default Setting</strong></td><td>Privacy is automatically protected</td><td>Users shouldn’t have to take action to protect their privacy—it should be built in and enabled by default.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Privacy Embedded into Design</strong></td><td>Privacy is a core design consideration</td><td>Privacy should be integrated into systems and processes from the outset—not added later as an afterthought.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Full Functionality – Positive-Sum, not Zero-Sum</strong></td><td>No trade-off between privacy and usability</td><td>Aim for solutions that support both privacy and other system goals (e.g., security, functionality) without unnecessary compromises.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>End-to-End Security – Full Lifecycle Protection</strong></td><td>Secure data throughout its entire lifecycle</td><td>Protect personal data from collection through to deletion using strong security practices (e.g., encryption, access controls).</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Visibility and Transparency – Keep it Open</strong></td><td>Be open and accountable</td><td>System operations and data handling practices should be transparent and verifiable to users and stakeholders.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Respect for User Privacy – Keep it User-Centric</strong></td><td>Put users in control of their data</td><td>Prioritize user interests, offer clear privacy options, and make systems easy to understand and control from a user perspective.</td></tr></tbody></table>
